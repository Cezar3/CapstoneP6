Initial Research

  1. Rates of childhood trauma are increasing in many American states because of the residual effects of covid.
  2. As a result of this issue kids across America need more resources in order to better understand the causes of their trauma and ways in which to fix it.
  3. Who is impacted by the problem? Children All over the world but mainly those who live in some sort of fear of losing an essential object of value within their life. Which includes the safety of them and their family or the comfortablility of their daily life.
  4. How many people are affected? To what extent are they impacted? Studies have shown that 20-25% of American children are affected by childhood trauma and suffer everlasting effects into their later life.
  5. How long has this issue persisted? This issue is as old as but only now are we gaining a grasp on what it is truly about and possibly even ways in which to fix it.

Key Data Points: Seemingly caused by the pandemic States including Pennsylvania, Ohio, Kentucky, Texas, Illinois, Michigan, and New York have seen a spike in child abuse. Support Organizations: FamilyForward, Prevent Child Abuse America. List of Identifiers of childhood trauma.
  • Accidents
  • Bullying/cyberbullying
  • Chaos or dysfunction in the house
  • Death of a loved one
  • Domestic violence
  • Emotional abuse or neglect
  • Incarcerated parent
  • Parent with a mental illness
  • Physical abuse or neglect
  • Separation from a parent or caregiver
  • Sexual abuse
  • Stress caused by poverty
  • Substance abuse
  • Sudden and/or serious medical condition
  • Violence (at home, at school, or in the surrounding community)
  • War/terrorism
